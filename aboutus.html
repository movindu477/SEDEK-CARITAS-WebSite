Caritas Sri Lanka – SEDEC

The social arm of the Catholic Bishops' Conference in Sri Lanka

Caritas Sri Lanka – SEDEC is an expression of the concern of the Catholic Church in Sri Lanka for justice, peace and human development. It operates on the mandate of the Catholic Bishops’ Conference in Sri Lanka (CBCSL) which is conferred to the Board of Management (BOM).

Caritas Sri Lanka – SEDEC is the National Secretariat of the Commission for Justice, Peace and Human Development of the Catholic Bishops’ Conference in Sri Lanka. In each Diocese, Caritas Sri Lanka – SEDEC has an implementing Caritas Diocesan Secretariat. These are the secretariats of the Commission for Justice, Peace & Human Development of the respective Dioceses.

Caritas Sri Lanka – SEDEC is independent of all political parties and ethnic groups. It draws its inspiration from the teachings of the Lord Jesus Christ, His unconditional love for one another and its mission is enrooted in the social doctrine of the Catholic Church.

Caritas Sri Lanka – SEDEC is a Member of Caritas Internationalis, which is an International Confederation of Catholic Organizations that comes under the purview of the Holy Father, mandated by their respective Episcopal Conferences to spread solidarity, social justice and integral human development.


LEGAL STATUS

Caritas Sri Lanka – SEDEC, attains its legal status under Parliamentary Act No. 17 of 1983 which is an Act to Incorporate the Catholic Bishops’ Conference in Sri Lanka.

This Act is cited as the Catholic Bishops’ Conference in Sri Lanka (Incorporation) Act No. 17 of 1983.

Rt. Rev. Dr. Harold Anthony Perera

Chairman

Rt. Rev. Dr. Harold Anthony Perera, born on 6 June 1951, is a respected Catholic Bishop in Sri Lanka. Ordained a priest in 1980, he has served as Bishop of Ratnapura, Galle, and Kurunegala. Becoming the first local diocesan bishop of Ratnapura, he is the current President of the Catholic Bishops' Conference of Sri Lanka and Chairman of Caritas Sri Lanka, recognized for his leadership in justice, peace, interfaith dialogue, and humanitarian service.

Rt. Rev. Dr. Anton Wyman Croos

Vice Chairman

Rt. Rev. Dr. Anton Wyman Croos is a dedicated Catholic Bishop serving the Sri Lankan community. Known for his deep commitment to faith, social justice, and pastoral care, he actively contributes to Caritas Sri Lanka's mission of uplifting communities through education, social welfare, and interreligious dialogue.

Rev. Fr. Luke Nelson Perera

National Director

Rev. Fr. Luke Nelson Perera has been a guiding force within Caritas Sri Lanka, leading with compassion and vision. His pastoral service and dedication to fostering social harmony have significantly strengthened Caritas' mission to serve the marginalized and promote unity among communities.
